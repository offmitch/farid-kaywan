Your How-to Guide to Refinancing Your Mortgage

Review Your Refinancing Options

You may want to add that new kitchen you’ve been dreaming about, take a special vacation, or even take care of your outstanding debt. To help you get there, you may want to reduce your term, switch from an adjustable-rate mortgage to a fixed-rate mortgage, or consolidate your first and second mortgages.

Check Your Credit

It’s a smart step to check your credit because lenders will do so. The better your credit rating, the more desirable your business will be to a lender. One easy step to start improving your credit rating is to avoid maxing out your credit card balances.

Go Shopping

Just like you shop for the best value on your groceries, your Mortgage Broker will go shopping on your behalf for the best interest rate and options. After all, a lower rate can save you big time in costs over the life of your new mortgage.

Prepare a List

Your Mortgage Broker will need some basic information to move forward, such as your current lender’s details, how much you owe on your current mortgage loan, your gross monthly income, and the value of your home.

Do the Math

You’ll be offered a number of loan options, so discuss them with your Mortgage Broker and choose one that best suits your needs. Beyond just considering the lowest rate, discuss whether a fixed or adjustable rate is better for your situation.

Factor in a Few Additional Costs

There will be a few fees and costs associated with this process, such as brokerage or underwriting fees, as well as transaction, settlement, and closing costs. Your Mortgage Broker can discuss these with you and factor them into the mortgage.

You're Ready to Apply

Okay, it’s time to apply with your chosen lender. Be ready with a few required documents, such as proof of income, income tax returns, recent bank statements, and your monthly expenses.

Home Sweet Home

That’s it! Once the paperwork is done, it’s time to take that vacation or begin your dream kitchen renovation. Enjoy!

A Solution Designed Specifically for Senior Homeowners

Reverse mortgages continue to gain popularity for Canadian homeowners aged 55 plus. One reason for the increased popularity is necessity—many retired Canadians seek ways to increase cash flow, pay off debts, or cover daily expenses. Additionally, many prefer to remain in their homes for as long as possible, which may require renovations and upkeep. Whatever your need, a reverse mortgage could be a great solution!

Who Offers Reverse Mortgages in Canada?

There are two Canadian Schedule I banks that offer reverse mortgages: Equitable Bank and Home Equity Bank. Both banks’ reverse mortgage products function similarly. We can help you determine which is better suited to your needs.

What is a Reverse Mortgage?

Reverse mortgages are designed for Canadians aged 55 and older, allowing them to access their home’s equity to improve financial stability. Unlike traditional mortgages, reverse mortgage borrowers are not required to make regular payments. Instead, the balance accrues interest and is only due when the homeowner sells or moves out.

Upon payout, the mortgage will include the original principal balance, accrued interest, and any fees incurred. However, historical data shows that home equity is typically maintained or grows due to appreciation values exceeding interest accrual.

While reverse mortgages primarily benefit older individuals, they can also help families looking to support elderly parents. Instead of selling and moving into care facilities, homeowners can use reverse mortgages to fund in-home care, maintaining their independence and comfort.

Reasons to Consider a Reverse Mortgage

Reverse mortgages allow homeowners to access up to 55% of their home’s equity, converting it into cash as a lump sum or structured monthly payments. Funds can be used to:

  • Pay off existing debts
  • Gift money to family
  • Enhance quality of life
  • Add safety features or renovations to the home
  • Expand an investment portfolio
  • Switch an existing mortgage dollar-for-dollar to eliminate payments and increase cash flow

Benefits of a Reverse Mortgage

Beyond tapping into home equity, reverse mortgages offer several advantages:

  • No monthly mortgage payments
  • No income or credit qualifications
  • Minimal paperwork required
  • Homeowners retain property title and ownership
  • Flexible options to break term early or make optional interest payments
  • Penalty waived in the event of death or care home placement to preserve the estate

Misconceptions about Reverse Mortgages

“With a Reverse Mortgage, You No Longer Own Your Home”

FALSE. You always maintain title, ownership, and control of your home. The lender simply has a first mortgage on the title.

“You Will Owe More Than the Value of Your Home”

FALSE. Most reverse mortgages include a “No Negative Equity Guarantee,” ensuring that as long as the homeowner meets obligations, the amount due will not exceed the fair market value of the home.

“Reverse Mortgages are Expensive”

FALSE. Costs associated with a reverse mortgage, such as appraisals and legal fees, are comparable to a traditional mortgage. The main difference is a one-time closing and administration fee.

“Reverse Mortgages Have Higher Interest Rates”

DEPENDS. While rates are slightly higher than traditional mortgages, the difference is minimal. Many retirees choose reverse mortgages because they may not qualify for a conventional mortgage or prefer the flexibility of no monthly payments.

“You Can’t Pass on Your Home”

FALSE. Your heirs can inherit the property by paying off the reverse mortgage after you pass away. Many reverse mortgage contracts include a “No Negative Equity Guarantee,” ensuring that if the mortgage balance exceeds the home's value, the lender covers the difference.
